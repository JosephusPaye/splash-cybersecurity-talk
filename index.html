Cybersecurity Hygiene

Simple habits to keep Splash of Colour Swimming
safe from cyber threats

Protecting your participants, your community, and your mission

Why does this matter for Splash of Colour?

  • 43% of cyberattacks target small organisations — non-profits are not exempt
  • You handle sensitive data every day: participant personal details, contact information, and private operational data for Splash
  • The community trusts you with their personal information — a breach can damage that trust and the reputation of Splash programs
  • The good news: most attacks are preventable with simple everyday habits

1 Use 1Password for All Your Passwords

  • Splash of Colour has 1Password set up for you — use it for every work account
  • Let 1Password generate your passwords — long, random, and unique without you having to remember them
  • If you've been saving passwords in Chrome or Edge — stop. Browser password managers are not as secure as 1Password, and having passwords scattered makes them harder to manage

↓ more details

1 1Password — Details

  • Never reuse a password across accounts — if one gets breached, the others stay safe
  • Never share passwords over email, text, or chat — use 1Password's secure sharing instead
  • Remove saved passwords from Chrome/Edge and keep everything in 1Password

↓

81% of data breaches involve weak or reused passwords. If your Microsoft 365 password is the same as your SMS system password, one breach of the SMS system exposes participant records too.
Make sure 1Password is installed on your devices and you're using it for all your Splash of Colour accounts.

2 Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

  • MFA adds a second layer of protection beyond your password — like a deadlock on top of a regular lock
  • When you log in, you'll confirm with a one-time code from an app or a notification on your phone
  • Even if someone steals your password, they still can't get in without your second factor

↓ more details

Without MFA, a stolen password = full account access. Phishing attacks regularly harvest passwords, but MFA stops them cold.
If you don't have it already or you're unsure, let me know and we'll check and enable MFA on your Microsoft 365 account.

3 Spot Phishing & Scams

  • Phishing = fake emails, texts, or messages designed to trick you into clicking a link or giving up information
  • Watch for: urgency ("act now!"), unexpected attachments, misspellings in sender addresses, and requests for passwords or payment
  • When in doubt, don't click. Verify with the sender through a different channel

↓ more details

3 Phishing — Details

91% of cyberattacks start with a phishing email. One click on a malicious link can compromise your account and the data you have access to.
Got a suspicious email? Don't click anything. Forward it to me (websites@josephuspaye.com) and delete it.

4 Keep Your Software Up to Date

  • When your computer or phone asks to update — save your work and say yes — don't keep hitting "remind me later"
  • Updates patch security holes that attackers actively exploit
  • Malwarebytes is installed on your devices — make sure it's running and don't disable or ignore it

↓ more details

4 Updates — Details

  • This applies to everything: Windows (your laptop), your phone, browsers, Microsoft 365 apps, and any other apps you use for work

↓

Unpatched systems are the #1 way attackers get in. Security software like Malwarebytes can only protect you if it's actually running — disabled or outdated protection is the same as no protection.
Next time you see an update notification, install it straight away. If it needs a restart, save your work and restart.

5 Lock Your Screen & Log Out Properly

  • Walk away from your device? Lock it first — just press Win + L
  • At the end of the day, log out of the computer — don't just close the lid
  • Make sure your phone has a PIN, fingerprint, or face unlock — no swipe-only locks

↓ more details

5 Lock & Log Out — Details

An unlocked device or a session left logged in overnight means anyone with access to that device can get into your accounts, participant information, and Splash documents.
Stepping away = lock screen. End of day = log out. Make it a habit.

6 Be Careful on Public Wi-Fi

  • Pool facilities, community centres, and cafes have open Wi-Fi that anyone can join — including attackers
  • Use your phone's mobile hotspot instead, or a trusted VPN if you must use public Wi-Fi

↓ more details

6 Public Wi-Fi — Details

  • Avoid accessing sensitive information (email, participant records, other Splash online systems) on public Wi-Fi

↓

Attackers can set up fake Wi-Fi networks with names like "Pool Free WiFi" to intercept everything you do online — including passwords and messages.
Next time you're working outside the office, use your phone's hotspot instead of connecting to public Wi-Fi.

7 Save Files to OneDrive, Not Your Desktop

  • Save all work files to OneDrive, not your desktop or Downloads folder
  • Never email participant records or sensitive documents to personal email accounts
  • Share files using OneDrive links instead of emailing attachments where possible

↓ more details

7 OneDrive — Details

  • Files in OneDrive are backed up, protected, and accessible from any device if yours breaks or gets lost
  • Sharing via links means access can be revoked if something goes wrong (e.g. a link mistakenly sent to the wrong person)

↓

Files saved only on your desktop disappear if your laptop is lost, stolen, or breaks. Participant or Splash data emailed to a personal Gmail account has left the safety of Splash of Colour's systems entirely.
Check your Desktop and Downloads folders now — move any work files to OneDrive.

8 Report Anything Suspicious

  • Weird email? Something acting strange? Tell Ennia immediately and forward it to me at websites@josephuspaye.com
  • There's no such thing as a silly question — it's always better to ask
  • Speed matters: the faster a threat is reported, the less damage it can do

↓ more details

8 Reporting — Details

  • If you think you clicked a bad link or entered your password somewhere suspicious — let Ennia know right away, then change your password on that account and save the new password in 1Password

↓

The average data breach goes undetected for 200+ days. Early reporting can be the difference between a close call and a serious incident.
Save my email somewhere easy to find (websites@josephuspaye.com). When in doubt, reach out.

Your Cybersecurity Checklist

1 Use 1Password — not your browser — for all passwords
2 Enable MFA on your Microsoft 365 account
3 Think before you click — verify suspicious messages
4 Install updates and keep Malwarebytes running
5 Lock your screen when away, log out at end of day
6 Use your phone's hotspot instead of public Wi-Fi
7 Save work files to OneDrive, not your desktop
8 Report anything suspicious to Ennia and Josephus

You don't need to be perfect.

You just need to be better than yesterday.

These habits are simple, but they make a real difference.
Every step you take keeps your participants, your community, and Splash of Colour safer.
